POSITION: Social Work Practicum Student at Carr Lane VPA MS

 

The St. Louis Public Schools District (SLPS) seeks a highly motivated and professional social work practicum student who has strong one-on-one and group facilitation skills and a passion for youth-centered programming. The practicum student will work closely with the Coordinator of Social Services and Carr Lane Visual and Performing Arts (CLVPA) leadership to provide social service support to students assigned to their caseload under a school-embedded project, The Initiative for Education Achievement and Opportunity (The Initiative). Mobilized by the Office of Students in Transition (SIT) and CLVPA leadership, The Initiative seeks to increase the academic success of youth highly impacted by homelessness through relationship-building strategies and supports that increase student sense-of-belonging.

 

The ideal candidate should understand the mission of the SLPS District and be able to articulate our visions and goals. The St. Louis Public Schools District works to provide a quality education for all students and enable them to realize their full potential.

 

Reports to: Gayle Abram (School Social Worker), Velva Hollimon (Coordinator of Social Services)

 

Responsibilities:

●      Plan and facilitate one-on-one and small group sessions related to peer-to-peer relationships, self-regulation techniques, and prosocial skill development

●      Serve as an active advocate for students’ social-emotional and physical wellbeing

●      Track and maintain student growth, as identified by progress notes, educator referrals, and weekly report of all activities

●      Support teacher professional development sessions related to rapport building and relationship-driven instructional strategies

●      Increase student and family knowledge/awareness of school and community resources to meet need

●      Provide referrals to school and community resources when appropriate

●      Attend weekly meetings with Field Instructor for supervision

 

Education and Experiences:

●      Bachelor’s Degree in Social Work, Education, Public Policy or another applicable field required

●      Preference for previous work experience with high-risk youth

●      Demonstrated knowledge of social work competencies and standards, including mandated reporting, confidentiality, and strong one-on-one and group facilitation skills

●      Ability to work, problem solve, and make decisions with a minimum of direct supervision

●      Strong interpersonal and communication (both written and oral) skills
